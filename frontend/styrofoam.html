Styrofoam Cup Not Curbside Recyclable

Because it is mostly air and prone to breaking into tiny pieces, Styrofoam is typically not processed at standard recycling facilities. It usually requires specialized drop-off locations or must be placed in the general trash.

Key Info

Category: Foam

Type: Expanded Polystyrene (EPS)

Common Use: Coffee cups, takeout boxes, egg cartons

How to Handle / Dispose

1 Empty out all liquid or food contents
2 Search for a specialized local EPS drop-off center
3 If no center exists, bag securely to prevent flying debris
4 Place in your general household trash bin

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • ❌ Do not put in your curbside recycling bin (it contaminates other recyclables)
  • ❌ Do not break into small pieces (creates microplastics that are hard to clean up)
  • ❌ Do not burn or microwave (releases toxic fumes)

Find Specialized Centers

Locate specialized facilities that can process expanded polystyrene

Additional Info

Why isn't it accepted curbside?

Styrofoam is 95% air, making it very light but bulky. It is expensive to transport and most standard recycling sorting machines cannot process it, as it breaks apart and contaminates paper and plastic streams.

Environmental impact

Styrofoam takes hundreds of years to decompose in a landfill. When littered, it easily breaks down into microplastics that can be ingested by wildlife and marine life.

Alternative reuse ideas

Before throwing it away, consider reusing clean cups for mixing paints, crafting, or holding small hardware items like screws and nails.
