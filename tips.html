Recycling Tips & Guides

Practical, step-by-step guides to help you recycle more effectively at home and at work.

Basics

The golden rule: when in doubt, leave it out

Contamination is one of the biggest challenges in recycling. A single non-recyclable item can ruin an entire load. If you are unsure whether an item is recyclable, it is better to put it in general waste than to contaminate a batch.

✓ Do

Rinse all containers before recycling
Flatten cardboard and plastic to save space
Sort by material type
Check local guidelines

✗ Don't

Wishful recycle unknown items
Bag recyclables in black bags
Mix food waste with dry recycling
Plastic

How to recycle plastic at home: a step-by-step guide

Plastic recycling works best when you identify the type, rinse it, and prepare it correctly before disposal.

✓ Do

Rinse bottles, jars, and tubs
Remove pumps and mixed-material parts
Check for type 1 or type 2 markings
Squash bottles to save space

✗ Don't

Recycle black plastic in kerbside bins
Include plastic film or bags
Recycle tiny pieces smaller than a credit card
E-Waste

Why e-waste is the fastest-growing waste stream

E-waste contains hazardous materials and valuable rare earth metals, so it should always go to a dedicated collection centre.

✓ Do

Use certified e-waste drop-off centres
Wipe personal data before donation
Ask about manufacturer take-back schemes
Donate working electronics

✗ Don't

Bin batteries
Leave personal data on old devices
Assume normal recycling bins accept electronics
Reduce

The recycling hierarchy: reduce and reuse come first

Recycling matters, but reducing what we buy and reusing items first has a bigger environmental impact.

✓ Do

Carry a reusable bottle and bag
Buy in bulk to reduce packaging
Repair before replacing
Donate or sell usable items

✗ Don't

Choose single-use items when alternatives exist
Throw away repairable electronics
Over-order products you may not use
